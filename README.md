Comprehensive Research Framework for AGB Estimation
This repository provides a robust Python-based research framework for forest Aboveground Biomass (AGB) estimation. The primary focus is a comparative analysis evaluating the efficacy of AlphaEarthFoundation embeddings against traditional multi-sensor remote sensing data streams, including Sentinel-1 (SAR), Sentinel-2 (Optical), and ALOS-2 PALSAR-2.
By integrating high-dimensional foundation model features with classical geospatial predictors, this study evaluates the frontier of self-supervised learning in ecological modeling.

Key Research Objectives
*Benchmark ML/DL Architectures: Investigate the predictive accuracy and computational efficiency of a diverse spectrum of models—ranging from ensemble-based machine learning to advanced deep learning architectures—specifically optimized for forest biomass quantification.

*Foundation vs. Traditional Data Analysis: Conduct a rigorous performance analysis to determine if foundation model embeddings (AlphaEarth) provide superior spatial representations and saturation-point mitigation compared to traditional multi-sensor radiometric and polarimetric data.

*Robustness & Cross-Validation: Evaluate model stability and generalization across a suite of architectures, including:

**Machine Learning: Random Forest (RF) and XGBoost.

**Neural Networks: Artificial Neural Networks (ANNs).

**Recurrent Architectures: Gated Recurrent Units (GRUs) and Bidirectional GRUs (BiGRUs) for temporal sequence modeling.

**Attention Mechanisms: Transformer-based Attention Encoders to capture long-range dependencies and feature interactions.

**Uncertainty & Explainability: Implement advanced validation frameworks to assess the robustness of biomass maps across diverse ecological zones and complex terrains.
